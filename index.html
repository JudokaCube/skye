  • You must follow Discord's own Terms of Service and Community Guidelines while using Skye.

  • Skye's economy (balances, XP, gambling games, etc.) is entirely virtual. It has no real-world value and can never be exchanged for real money or goods.
  • Skye is actively in development and does not have reliable hosting yet. Uptime is low, features may change, and data may occasionally be reset without notice.

Privacy Policy

What's collected. Skye stores only what it needs to run its features: Discord user, server and channel IDs, and — only for specific commands — small bits of content such as AFK messages, warning reasons, poll options, reminder text, anonymous confessions, or recently deleted messages used for .snipe.

How it's used. This data powers the feature you triggered — economy balances and leaderboards, XP and levels, reminders, warnings, AFK status, and the snipe cache. It is never sold, and it isn't shared with anyone for advertising or marketing.

Third-party lookups. Commands like .translate, .weather and .define send your query text to the relevant external API purely to fetch a result. Those requests are subject to that service's own privacy policy.

Retention. Temporary data (like the snipe cache) is cleared regularly or on restart. Persistent data (balances, XP, warnings, reminders) is kept until it's removed by a server admin, the affected user, or the developer.

Your choices. Server admins and users can request their data be deleted at any time by reaching out through .bugreport or opening an issue on GitHub.

Kids. Skye isn't directed at children under 13 (or Discord's own minimum age, whichever is higher), and doesn't knowingly collect anything beyond what Discord itself already provides.

Questions. For anything not covered here, message the developer through .bugreport or open an issue on the project's GitHub page.
